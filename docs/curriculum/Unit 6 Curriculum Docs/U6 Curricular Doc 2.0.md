**Unit 6 Curricular Document**

**Topic: Human Impacts to Watersheds/ Water Cycle**

**Learning Objectives:**

- Predict the spread of dissolved materials through a watershed.

- Explain movement of water from the surface system to the ground system

- Explain the movement of water between surface and atmospheric systems

- Identify and describe the human activities that have impacted the different water systems (i.e. surface, ground, atmospheric) on the planet

**Argumentation**

- Provide a counter-critique

  - Critique another argument. Fully explain the claim that the argument is flawed and provide justification for why that argument is flawed

**Driving Question:** Are the water systems on the planet functioning normally?

**Underlying Facts & Principles**

- Materials that dissolve in water will move with water while the water remains in a liquid state.

- Substances dissolved in water will be carried by liquid water from place to place.

- Gravity and topography govern where water flows in a watershed resulting in particular connected patterns of water flow across a watershed.

- Surface water infiltrates or moves through the soil into the ground

- Water infiltrates through the upper layers of soil and rock \[the unsaturated zone in with some spaces are filled with air and other spaces are filled with water\] until it reaches the water table.

- Water enters the atmosphere through evaporation from surface water, transpiration from plants, and evaporation from animals.

- Water is able to evaporate while other substances, such as salts, are not.

- Changes in phase refer to water vapor condensing into a liquid or liquid water evaporating to form water vapor or gaseous water.

**Learning Resources:**

1.  **Water Cycle:** Water is found all over Earth in the ocean, on land and in the atmosphere. The water cycle is the path that all water follows as it moves around the planet.

2.  **Evaporation**: Occurs when liquid water on the surface turns into water vapor in the atmosphere.

3.  **Condensation:** Warm water vapor rises up through the atmosphere. As the water vapor rises higher the cooler air of the atmosphere causes the water vapor to turn into liquid water, creating clouds. This process is called condensation

4.  **Ground Water:** Groundwater is the water found underground in the cracks and spaces in soil, sand, and rock

5.  **Surface Water:** Surface Water is the water above ground, including streams, rivers, lakes and the ocean

6.  **Atmospheric Water:** Water vapor in the atmosphere and air
