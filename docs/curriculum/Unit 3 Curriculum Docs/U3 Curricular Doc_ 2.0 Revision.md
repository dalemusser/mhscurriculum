**Unit 3: Pollution**

**Topic:** Tracking a soluble pollutant through a watershed.

**Learning Objectives**

**Water Systems**

> W3.1 Predict the spread of dissolved materials through a watershed.
>
> W3.2 Identify the direction of water flow based on a map of the watershed.
>
> **Argumentation**
>
> A3.1 Identify a warrant (reasoning).
>
> A3.2 Construct a warrant (reasoning) that links a claim with evidence.

**Driving Question:**

- Why will a pollutant reach some parts of the watershed while other parts of the same watershed are unaffected by the pollutant?

**Underlying Facts & Principles**

- Materials that dissolve in water will move with water while the water remains in a liquid state.

- Molecules of a dissolved solid are thoroughly mixed with water molecules and cannot be separated by physical means \[cannot be separated by filtration\].

- Chemical characteristics of water \[solvent\] and the dissolved solid \[solute\] are not altered when a solution is formed.

- Substances dissolved in water will be carried by liquid water from place to place.

- Gravity and topography govern where water flows in a watershed resulting in particular connected patterns of water flow across a watershed.

  - The pattern of water movement will be determined by the topography of the watershed.

  - This governs where surface pollutants will travel
