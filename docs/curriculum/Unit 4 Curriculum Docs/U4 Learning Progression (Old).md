**Learning Progression**

<u>Quest 1: Alien Village</u>

*Curricular Purpose: Introduce core vocabulary- water table, saturated/unsaturated zones, and how this relates to extracting water from underground*

1.  Establish need for a lake/pond/reservoir to pull water from

- Anderson is building a remote worksite and needs water- player tasked with finding it- she suggests exploring ruins

- *<span class="mark">Curricular Purpose: Reinforce the social/biological importance of water</span>*

2.  Locate the non-functional fountains and city ruins

- DANI suggests that in order for a city and water system to exist here there must have been a working water source nearby

- Prompted to check ruins

3.  Discover non-functioning drill/ well structure

- DANI ties the well to the idea of underground water source

- Try the controls but there is visible evidence that it needs power and that the power mechanism appears to come from a lower floor

4.  <span class="mark">Deliver Toppo Lesson [<u>U4 Toppo Lessons</u>](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1PmxuhpsTUeE_84r89jnzXxcz7-R1XPdqHolljx2qIP8/edit#slide=id.g275d16cb9f7_0_0)</span>

- <span class="mark">water table, infiltration, saturated vs unsaturated zones, aquifer?, deeper well = cleaner water, permeability</span>

- *Curricular Purpose: Deliver the formal definitions/concepts that set the basis for the rest of the unit*

<u>Quest 2: The Ruins</u>

Player takes elevator which malfunctions and leaves them stranded on bottom floor

1.  <span class="mark">7th Floor: Alien Glyph [<u>U4 Alien Glyphs</u>](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1Z_w0lwJydAfxBp8Gg3Al1pLluDeRjwA0d5QAHzhQ6mI/edit#slide=id.g2771bdee883_0_0)</span>

    - *<span class="mark">Curricular Purpose: First exposure to the concept that a relationship exists between soil type/permeability and infiltration rate</span>*

2.  6th Floor: Bedrock Level

    - Players are introduced to the soil particle view monitors

      1.  *Curricular Purpose: Explore the “why” of the relationship cited in “A”- deliver information on bedrock particle size and reinforce understanding of the water table.*

    - Players encounters first generator

      1.  This one is already set to correct water flow/ power if flowing through generator but not connected to door breaker

    - Players solve simple cube puzzle

      1.  Place cube on panel to turn on power to door

3.  5th Floor: Sand

    - Players are introduced to sand particle view

      1.  *Curricular Purpose: Explore the “why” of the relationship cited in “A” - deliver information on sand particle size and reinforce understanding of the water table.*

    - Players solve slightly more complex cube puzzle

      1.  Place missing pipe piece in place (reinforces that the generators are water powered)

      2.  Change water flow and float cube to next room

      3.  Place cube on pad

4.  4th Floor: Sand

    - Players encounter another sand particle view display (no new info delivered just reinforcement of past concepts)

    - Players solve more complex cube puzzle requiring infiltration knowledge

      1.  Encounters overflowing generator set to where water is flowing through gravel

      2.  Player must set to soil type to “sand” to slow water flow and get power going properly

          1.  *Curricular Purpose: Assess knowledge that sand has a slower infiltration rate than gravel.*

5.  3rd Floor: Gravel

    - Players encounter another gravel particle view display

      1.  *Curricular Purpose: Explore the “why” of the relationship cited in “A”- deliver information on gravel particle size/reinforce understanding of the water table.*

    - Players solve cube puzzle requiring infiltration knowledge

      1.  Player encounter 2 generators

          1.  Generator \#1

              1.  Players insert pipe into wall to start water flow which results in too much water flowing

              2.  Players must change soil type from gravel to sand?

                  1.  *Curricular Purpose: Assess knowledge that sand has a slower infiltration rate than gravel.*

          2.  Generator \#2

              1.  Players must change soil type from sand (or clay?) to gravel in order to increase water flow to power the generator

                  1.  *Curricular Purpose: Assess knowledge that sand (clay?) has a slower infiltration rate than gravel.*

      2.  Player puts cube on pad to power breaker

6.  2nd Floor: Clay

    - Players encounter clay particle view display

      1.  *Curricular Purpose: Explore the “why” of the relationship cited in “A”- deliver information on clay particle size/reinforce understanding of the water table.*

    - Player solves puzzle requiring infiltration knowledge

      1.  Player encounters 3 generators

          1.  Generator \#1

              1.  Set to bedrock but not getting enough water

              2.  Player sets to gravel to increase flow

                  1.  *Curricular Purpose: Assess knowledge that gravel has a higher infiltration rate than bedrock*

          2.  Generator \#2 (Left)

              1.  Set to sand but has too much flow

              2.  Set to bedrock to slow rate

                  1.  *Curricular Purpose: Assess knowledge that sand has a higher infiltration rate than bedrock*

          3.  Generator \#3 (Right)

              1.  Set to sand but doesn’t have enough flow

              2.  Player sets to gravel to increase flow

                  1.  *Curricular Purpose: Assess knowledge that gravel has a higher infiltration rate than sand*

7.  1st Floor: Sand

    - Player puts cube on pad to power breaker?

<u>Quest 3: The Fountain</u>

- Turn on the drill

  1.  Player selects correct layer to drill well into the water table

  2.  *Curricular Purpose: Assess student understanding of infiltration and the water table through correct well placement*

<u>Quest 4-8: Flooding the Bunker-Checking the Warehouse</u>

Anderson says the bunker power is now out and she tasks the player with figuring out why

1.  Player Collects Evidence

- *Curricular Purpose: Provide evidence for later argumentation*

  1.  Level 1: Armory (FLOODED)

      1.  Sand above/ clay below

      2.  20 meters deep

  2.  Level 2: Living Quarters (not flooded)

      1.  Clay above and below

      2.  30 meters deep

  3.  Level 3: Mess Hall (not flooded)

      1.  Clay above/ sand below

  4.  Level 4: Warehouse (FLOODED)

      1.  Sand above/ bedrock below

      2.  50 meters deep

<u>Quest 9: Argue about Flooded Bunker</u>

1.  Player completes argument \#1 (Who flooded the armory?)

- *Curricular Purpose: Students generate a complete argument*

2.  Player completes argument \#2 (Who flooded the warehouse?)

- *Curricular Purpose: Students identify/critique a faulty argument*

<u>Quest 10-11: Fix the Leak- Anderson’s Request</u>

1.  Player fixes leak by turning of fountain near bunker

2.  Now that the fountains are working, Anderson requests the players help in making a lake for the remote working site to pull water from

    1.  Player collects evidence on three possible sites identified by Anderson
