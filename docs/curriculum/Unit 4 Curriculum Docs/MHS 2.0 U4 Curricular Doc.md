# Unit 4: Groundwater

## Curricular Topic

Tracking the movement of water from the surface of the earth through soil and into groundwater.

## Learning Objectives

### Water Systems

> W4.1 Predict the location of the water table based on the saturated (region below the water table where all open spaces are filled with water) and unsaturated zones (region immediately below the land surface that contains air and water).
>
> W4.2 Predict rates of infiltration based on the permeability of the underlying substrate.
>
> W4.3 Explain movement of water from the surface system to the ground system.

###  Argumentation

> A4.1 Students will generate a complete argument (claim, reasoning, evidence)

## Underlying Facts & Principles

- Surface water infiltrates or moves through the soil into the ground

- The rate of infiltration is affected by the permeability and porosity of the soil and underlying rock layers. As soil particle size increases, the rate of infiltration increases.

- Water infiltrates through the upper layers of soil and rock \[the unsaturated zone in with some spaces are filled with air and other spaces are filled with water\] until it reaches the water table.

## Backing Info

1)  Infiltration: The process by which water on the ground surface enters the soil.

2)  Water Table: The level below which the ground is saturated (space between soil particles is filled with water)

3)  Soil Type: Water will infiltrate differently based on the type of soil. Water will move fastest through sand, and slowest through clay. Water moves through sand at a slower rate than gravel and a faster rate than clay.
