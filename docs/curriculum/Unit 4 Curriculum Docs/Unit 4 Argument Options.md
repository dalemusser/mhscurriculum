## Scenario One

- Unit begins with Anderson telling player about two alien ruins she’s found in the area. 
- She excitedly tells the player about using her new AI (messed up version DANI) to  clean up the interior of the second set of ruins. 
- Anderson asks the player to fix the fountains while she continues to work. 
- Immediately after the player turns on the fountains they hear a huge  explosion  in the area of the second ruins. (Anderson’s deficient AI has set off bombs inside each room to “clean” them)  
- They get a call from Anderson about flooding/water leaking into the bottom floor of ruin #2 
- Anderson admits that there are now holes in the floor/ceiling of each ruin room but  insists  that there must be a leak in the fountain and that turning it off will stop the flooding issue-
- She  stubbornly  continues her work in the bottom floor of the ruins. Player turns off fountain.  
- Player must argue whether or not the flooding will resolve after turning off the fountain/ is Anderson safe or in danger

## Possible Argument #1

Claim

The flooding will resolve on its own after the fountain is turned off.

The flooding will NOT resolve on its own after the fountain is turned off.

Evidence 

There is sand above the warehouse.

There is bedrock below the warehouse 

Reasoning 

Water infiltrates through sand easily; therefore, water from the fountain moved through the  soil  quickly and into the warehouse.

Water is seeping into the warehouse because the surrounding soil is filled with water and water cannot infiltrate through the bedrock.  Since there is bedrock below the warehouse water will continue to pool around the warehouse even if the fountain is turned off. 

Water in the warehouse will infiltrate through the bedrock layer, leaving the warehouse dry. 

Water from the water table will be able to infiltrate up through the space between the soil molecules into the sandy layer above the warehouse.
