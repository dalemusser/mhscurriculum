Unit 4: Ground Water

**Topic:** Tracking the movement of water from the surface of the earth through soil and into groundwater.

**Learning Objectives:**

**Water Systems**

> 4.1 Predict the location of the water table based on the saturated (region below the water table where all open spaces are filled with water) and unsaturated zones (region immediately below the land surface that contains air and water).
>
> 4.2 Predict rates of infiltration based on the permeability of the underlying substrate.

4.3 Explain movement of water from the surface system to the ground system.

**Underlying Facts & Principles**

- Surface water infiltrates or moves through the soil into the ground

- The rate of infiltration is affected by the permeability and porosity of the soil and underlying rock layers.

- Water infiltrates through the upper layers of soil and rock \[the unsaturated zone in with some spaces are filled with air and other spaces are filled with water\] until it reaches the water table.

**Task Objectives:** [<u>Scenario Overview</u>](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1K1dy0LMAvyOsbH6GRXKB6GsepQy6lzO5-4Dy5vVsVGw/edit)

<u>Task 1: Locate the fountain ruins, get the water flowing again</u>

At a point of interest, the player will find some ancient ruins, complete with a dried-up water source and above ground irrigation system. The waterway system starts at an ancient fountain. When the player visits this location they are prompted by ARF to explore the area. The player is told that in order for a city and water system to have been built here, there must have been a working water source nearby. Once they find the fountain there is some dialogue about finding out what happened to the water. The player will then scan the lake in 2D and look for the water table below.

<u>Task 2: Solve the Warehouse and Armory Floods</u>

Argument 1: Warehouse Flood

The player then receives a call from someone working below the ruins. The military has been digging an underground bunker and now finds that their underground warehouse has begun to flood. The player is accused of causing the flooding due to having fixed the water system above ground and the player must argue back that, in fact, the military has made a mistake. In error they have dug the bunker such that it part of it is located below the water table in the saturated zone.

Argument 2: Armory Flood

After solving the warehouse flood, the player is informed of additional flooding in a different part of the bunker. The new flooding is occurring in the Armory, and the player again is again being accused of the flooding due the activation of the fountain. Through argumentation the player assumed responsibility for the flooding.

**Backing Info:**

1)  Infiltration: The process by which water on the ground surface enters the soil.

(Training Video) Portable Water Filtration--can use sand to filter water--(different models for different types of soil--clay or sand)

2\) Water Table: The level below which the ground is saturated with water

(Training Video) Failure of well due to insufficient depth- important to find the correct depth

3\) Soil Type: Water will infiltrate differently based on the type of soil. Water will move fastest through sand, and slowest through clay. Water moves through silt as a slower rate than sand and a faster rate than clay.
