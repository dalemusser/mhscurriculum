Standards - Overview of Science Curriculum

**OVERVIEW OF MISSION HYDROSCI SCIENCE CURRICULUM**

<span class="mark">Mission HydroSci (MHS) teaches earth science concepts at a level at is appropriate for middle school students. Earth Science</span> <span class="mark">is an integrated science, bringing together chemistry, biology, and physics as they apply to the workings of the Earth. The</span> <span class="mark">applied and often visual nature of Earth Science helps students make connections to their lives and communities. The</span> <span class="mark">curriculum of MHS focuses on the water systems component of Earth Science. MHS consists of various curriculum units are</span> <span class="mark">each designed to teach a major topic within water systems. These major topics include: surface water, water pollution,</span> <span class="mark">groundwater, and atmospheric water. As students play through MHS, they will complete tasks that require knowledge of</span> w<span class="mark">ater systems. These tasks range from findings the source of pollution within a watershed to navigating through an</span> underground puzzle using their knowledge of evaporation and condensation.

<span class="mark">In addition to teaching students about water systems content, the MHS curriculum also focuses on building students’</span> <span class="mark">argumentation skills. Within each unit, students are given the opportunity to formulate an argument by selecting</span> <span class="mark">appropriate and claim, reasoning and evidence. Within each unit, player will collect data about the planet and use their</span> <span class="mark">collected data to support their arguments. Throughout the game players will argue about a range of topics from where to</span> s<span class="mark">et up base camp based on the size of surrounding watersheds to the cause of a flood in an underground bunker based on</span> <span class="mark">data about soil profile. The MHS curriculum emphasizes an understanding of water systems knowledge, as well as applying</span> science knowledge in the argumentation tasks.

**WHAT IS IT?**

The MHS curriculum has a strong focus on argumentation, and the argumentation system allows players to construct an argument by selecting a claim, reasoning, and evidence. Each unit has an argumentation task that allows students to build arguments with non-player characters in the game to move the story forward. The argumentation systems provide feedback to help players improve their arguments.

**WHAT ARE PUZZLE DUNGEONS?**

Within the game, players will encounter puzzle dungeons. The dungeons task players with finding a way to unlock doors and advance to the next room. There are two puzzle dungeon in the game and each focuses on a specific water systems concept.

**HOW TO USE**

Once players have built a

complete argument with a claim, reasoning, and evidence they click the submit button. If a player’s submitted argument is not sufficient to complete the tasks, the players will receive feedback that provides a hint of how they can improve their argument.

**HOW TO SUBMIT ARGUMENTS**

Players can submit their

arguments as much as they like until they have built a sufficient argument to advance to the next task. While in the argumentation system, players can access collected backing information to help them make sense of the data they have collected within the unit, and the science content that was covered within the unit.

**WHERE ARE THEY?**

The dungeon in Unit 4 focuses on infiltration, as players adjust the soil type to control the rate of infiltration to activate generators that can power on the doors In Unit 5, players will use either condensers or evaporators to transfer water between the liquid and gaseous phase.

Upon investigating the alien ruins the player finds an ancient drill room that was used by the aliens to obtain water from the ground. **How does the soil puzzle work?**

**A:** The soil puzzle works by allowing you to change the soil type that water moves through. By changing the soil type, you will notice that the level of water in the lock changes. These changes are due to the fact that water moves through different types of soils at different rates. For

example, water moves much faster through sand than clay.

**STUDENT TIPS FOR THIS UNIT**

**How do I find the water table?**

**A:** The water table is the space underground in which the soil is completely saturated (water present between small soil particles) with water. The boundary of this space is area underground where soil that is saturated is next to soil that is unsaturated (water not present between small soil particles)

> **A:** Humidity is a measure of the amount of water that is in the air. Higher humidity means there is more gaseous water in the air, and low humidity means there is less water in the air.

> **A:** Bill’s salt process works by using both condensation and evaporation. When you evaporate salt water, only
