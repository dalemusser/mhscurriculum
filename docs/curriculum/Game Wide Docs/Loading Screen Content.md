## Unit 4

19. Surface water infiltrates, or moves, through the soil into the ground.

20. The rate at which water moves through soil depends on the size of the spaces between its particles.

21. Water will move faster through soil with large pore spaces between its particles.

22. Water will move slower through soil with small pore spaces between its particles.

23. Soil with larger particles doesn’t hold onto water well because water moves quickly through its pore spaces.

24. Soil with smaller particles holds onto water very well because water clings to the surface of the particles and prevents it from flowing through quickly.

25. As water infiltrates into the ground and moves through more and more layers, the soil filters pollutants, and the water becomes cleaner.

26. Soil is said to be “saturated” when all the spaces between soil particles are filled with water.

27. The water table is the boundary below which soil is completely saturated with water.

28. Wells can be dug into the ground to make groundwater accessible on the surface.

## Unit 5

29. The water cycle includes surface water, groundwater, and water in the atmosphere.

30. Water enters the atmosphere through evaporation from surface water, transpiration from plants, and evaporation from animals.

31. The movement of water into the atmosphere requires a phase change from liquid to gas.

32. For water to change phases, energy from heat must be either added or removed.

33. Evaporation is a phase change that occurs when energy is added to a liquid to turn it into a gas.

34. Condensation is a phase change that occurs when energy is removed from a gas to turn it into a liquid.

35. Precipitation occurs when liquid water molecules in the air clump together and become heavy enough to fall from the sky.

36. Water never disappears from the water cycle- but it does change forms through evaporation and condensation.
