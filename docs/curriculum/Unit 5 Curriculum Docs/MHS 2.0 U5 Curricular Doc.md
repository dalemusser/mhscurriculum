**Unit 5: Atmospheric Water**

**Topic:** The movement of water through a cycle focusing on changes of state within atmospheric water and water on the earth’s surface.

**Water Science Objective:**

<u>W5.1</u> Explain the movement of water between surface and atmospheric systems

**Argumentation Objective:**

<u>A5.1</u> Provide a counter argument to a faulty claim that is provided .

**Driving Question:**

- What is the interaction between energy and water as it travels through surface and atmospheric systems

**Underlying Facts & Principles**

- Atmospheric water is part of the water cycle.

- Water enters the atmosphere through evaporation from surface water, transpiration from plants, and evaporation from animals.

- The movement of water into the atmosphere requires a phase change \[liquid state into a gaseous state\].

- Changes in phase refer to water vapor condensing into a liquid or liquid water evaporating to form water vapor or gaseous water.

- Water is able to evaporate while other substances, such as salts, are not.

- Changes in altitude cause a change in temperature(energy) and therefore phase.

| Water only evaporates from large bodies such as oceans or seas. | Water evaporates from all living things including plants and animals as well as all bodies of water: puddles, streams, rivers, etc., and even soil (Brody, 1993).  |
