**Key Concepts**

- When water vapor is cooled it condenses to form liquid water. This decreases the humidity in the room.

- When liquid water is heated it evaporates to form water vapor. This increases the humidity in the room.

- Water flows from areas of high elevation to areas of low elevation. (this is in just a subtle reinforcement of U3 objective 3.2 and not a primary objective)

**2.0 Curricular Updates**

Place more emphasis overall on temperature change- the humidity aspect is less important- see assessment questions below for context.

- In 1.0 the player only manipulates the cubes/ sets them on the pads to turn on condenser/evaporator- then they view the resulting change in humidity. If we stick with this mechanic we should call out the resulting temperature change primarily (we can retain the humidity readings as well but that’s more of a bonus idea)
