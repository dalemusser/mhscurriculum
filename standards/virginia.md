# Virginia Standards of Learning (SOL) — Science

## About

Virginia's science standards are the **Science Standards of Learning (SOL)**, most recently revised in **2018**. Standards are published by the [Virginia Department of Education](https://www.doe.virginia.gov/teaching-learning-assessment/k-12-standards-instruction/science/standards-of-learning).

Virginia is **not an NGSS state.** SOL is its own framework and predates NGSS; the 2018 revision adds three-dimensional language but retains Virginia-specific scope and code system.

**Code format:** `<grade>.<number>` with sub-letters for elaborations — e.g. `6.8.a`. Standards are organized by individual grade.

**Grade-level themes:**
- Grade 6 — Watershed-focused; substantial alignment to MHS
- Grade 7 — Life science (less MHS overlap)
- Grade 8 — Physical science + Earth/space (atmosphere, climate)

---

## SOL Standards Aligned to MHS Content

### 6.8 — Watersheds *(STRONG match — Unit 2 / Unit 3)*

> **The student will investigate and understand that land and water have roles in watershed systems.**

Sub-elaborations include: components of a watershed; how water flows through a watershed; how human activities affect watersheds; importance of conservation.

**MHS objectives served:** `W2.1`, `W2.2`, `W3.1`, `W3.2`, partial `W4.1`–`W4.3`.

This is one of the strongest single-state-standard fits for MHS in any U.S. system — it foregrounds watersheds the way MHS does.

### 6.7 — Air and Atmosphere *(Unit 5 framing)*

Atmospheric composition, patterns, and the water cycle are addressed under grade 6 elaborations.

**MHS objectives served:** `W5.1`.

### 6.9 — Public Policy / Resource Conservation *(Unit 6)*

Grade 6 includes a benchmark on the role of public policy in environmental issues and personal/societal stewardship.

**MHS objectives served:** Unit 6 (human impacts on water systems).

### 8.7 / 8.8 — Earth and Space *(secondary)*

Grade 8 covers atmospheric/oceanic interactions and Earth's surface processes.

### 6.1 / 7.1 / 8.1 — Scientific Investigation, Reasoning, and Logic

Each grade's `.1` standard covers scientific practices including argumentation. These are the touchpoints for MHS's argumentation arc.

**MHS objectives served:** All argumentation objectives.

---

## Notable Differences from NGSS

1. **Watershed is a dedicated standard** (`6.8`) rather than dispersed; this is actually closer to MHS's framing than NGSS is.
2. **No infiltration-mechanics standard** at the depth NGSS or MLS specifies.
3. **Argumentation is in the Scientific Investigation strand**, not woven through content the way NGSS does.
4. **Standards are by individual grade**, with a tighter focus per grade than NGSS's grade-band approach.

## Recommended Alignment Strategy

- **Grade 6 is by far the strongest fit** — `6.8` (watersheds) is essentially MHS's Units 2–3 reframed.
- Use `6.7` for Unit 5 and `6.9` for Unit 6 framing.
- Use `6.1` / `7.1` / `8.1` for argumentation-arc claims.

## Adoption & Status

- **Current standards:** Science Standards of Learning (2018).
- **Implementation:** Tested via SOL assessments in grades 5 and 8; revised assessments first administered spring 2023.
