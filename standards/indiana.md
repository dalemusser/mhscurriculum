# Indiana Academic Standards for Science

## About

Indiana's current science standards are the **Indiana Academic Standards for Science**, adopted by the Indiana State Board of Education on **June 8, 2022**. The 2022 standards are explicitly based on the **Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS)** and the *A Framework for K-12 Science Education* (NRC, 2012). Standards are published by the [Indiana Department of Education (IDOE)](https://www.in.gov/doe/students/indiana-academic-standards/science-and-computer-science/).

The 2022 adoption is **a substantive shift toward NGSS** — replacing the prior 2016 Indiana Academic Standards for Science (which were Indiana-specific, not NGSS-based).

**Code format:** Indiana uses NGSS-style PE codes wrapped in Indiana-specific organization. For MHS alignment purposes, the NGSS PE codes in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** apply.

## MHS Alignment

Because Indiana's 2022 standards are NGSS-based, the alignment in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** applies to Indiana with minor branding differences. The five directly cited PEs, the five adjacent PEs, and the SEP-7 argumentation thread all map to Indiana standards in the corresponding three-dimensional structure.

## Adoption Details

- **Adopted:** June 8, 2022 (Indiana State Board of Education).
- **Local name:** Indiana Academic Standards for Science.
- **Modifications:** NGSS-based with Indiana editorial choices and state framing; the underlying PEs are NGSS-equivalent.
- **Prior version:** Indiana Academic Standards for Science (2016) — Indiana-specific, not NGSS-based.
- **Notable:** The 2022 update significantly improved coverage of evolution and climate change compared to 2016.
