# Nebraska's College and Career Ready Standards for Science (NCCRS-S)

## About

Nebraska's current science standards are **Nebraska's College and Career Ready Standards for Science (NCCRS-S)**, approved by the Nebraska State Board of Education on **September 8, 2017**. Standards are published by the [Nebraska Department of Education](https://www.education.ne.gov/science/standards-and-supporting-documents/).

NCCRS-S is **NGSS-influenced** but uses Nebraska-specific framing. Each standard begins with a common stem: *"Gather, analyze, and communicate…"* — emphasizing scientific communication as a foundational practice.

**Grade structure:** Individual grades K–8; grade-span at high school. Standards within each grade are organized by topics.

## MHS Alignment

NCCRS-S addresses water cycle, watersheds, human impact, and argumentation in middle school grades; conceptual alignment to NGSS PEs maps via Nebraska's standards documentation. The MHS alignment in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** applies through PE-by-PE crosswalk.

## Adoption Details

- **Adopted:** September 8, 2017 (Nebraska State Board of Education).
- **Stem language:** "Gather, analyze, and communicate..." opens each standard.
