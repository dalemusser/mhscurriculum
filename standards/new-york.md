# New York State Science Learning Standards (NYSSLS)

## About

New York adopted the **New York State P-12 Science Learning Standards (NYSSLS)** in December 2016, effective **July 1, 2017**. Standards are published by the [New York State Education Department (NYSED)](https://www.nysed.gov/standards-instruction/science).

NYSSLS is **NGSS-based with a small set of New-York-specific additions** (~14 additional standards across grades K–12 to address topics the state determined needed more emphasis). New York was a [lead state in NGSS development](https://www.nextgenscience.org/lead-state-new-york), so NYSSLS is closer to direct NGSS adoption than most non-NGSS-direct states.

**Code format:** Standard NGSS PE codes (`MS-ESS2-4`, etc.) for the NGSS-derived standards. NY-specific additions use NY-prefixed codes.

## MHS Alignment

The five directly-cited NGSS PEs and five adjacent PEs documented in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** all apply to NYSSLS verbatim — New York adopts those PEs unchanged. The argumentation/SEP-7 mapping in `ngss.md` also applies as-is.

NYSSLS-specific additions emphasize topics like climate change and human/environmental interactions that align well with MHS Unit 6 framing but do not introduce new alignment requirements for the core MHS curriculum.

## Adoption Details

- **Adopted:** December 2016 (NY Board of Regents)
- **Effective:** July 1, 2017
- **Implementation:** Initial transition began 2017–2018 school year; full implementation phased through 2024.
- **Local name:** New York State P-12 Science Learning Standards (NYSSLS)
- **Modifications:** Added ~14 NY-specific standards across K–12; core NGSS PEs unchanged.
