# Pennsylvania STEELS Standards — Science, Technology & Engineering, Environmental Literacy & Sustainability

## About

Pennsylvania's current science standards are the **STEELS Standards (Science, Technology & Engineering, Environmental Literacy & Sustainability)** — adopted January 2022 by the Pennsylvania State Board of Education, published as final in the Pennsylvania Bulletin July 16, 2022. Standards are administered by the [Pennsylvania Department of Education](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/education/programs-and-services/instruction/elementary-and-secondary-education/curriculum/science/science-standards).

STEELS is **NGSS-aligned with three-dimensional learning** (Disciplinary Core Ideas, Science and Engineering Practices, Crosscutting Concepts) and explicitly integrates environmental literacy and sustainability content alongside the NGSS framework.

**Code format:** PA codes use a combined identifier per standard. The underlying PE structure mirrors NGSS, so for MHS alignment purposes the NGSS PE codes in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** apply.

## MHS Alignment

STEELS is NGSS-based. The five directly cited PEs, the five adjacent PEs, and the argumentation/SEP-7 mapping in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** apply directly to PA. STEELS's environmental-literacy-and-sustainability emphasis strengthens the alignment to MHS Unit 6 (human impacts on water systems).

## Adoption Details

- **Adopted:** January 2022 (PA State Board of Education)
- **Published as final:** July 16, 2022 (Pennsylvania Bulletin)
- **Implementation:** Three-year window for transition; full classroom integration by 2025–2026 school year.
- **Sunset of prior standards:** The 2002 Academic Standards for Science and Technology and 2002 Environment and Ecology standards sunset June 30, 2025.

## Notable Features

- **Integrated environmental literacy** — STEELS uses environmental, ecological, and sustainability contexts to frame science learning. MHS's planet-stranded survival narrative and Unit 6 human-impacts focus map naturally to this framing.
- **Three-dimensional approach** — disciplinary core ideas, science/engineering practices, and crosscutting concepts are explicit (matches NGSS).
- **Engineering integrated with science** — like NGSS, engineering practices are built into science standards rather than separated.
