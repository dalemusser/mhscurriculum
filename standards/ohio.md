# Ohio's Learning Standards for Science

## About

Ohio's science standards are **Ohio's Learning Standards and Model Curriculum for Science**, adopted **February 2018**. Standards are published by the [Ohio Department of Education and Workforce](https://education.ohio.gov/Topics/Learning-in-Ohio/Science).

Ohio is **not an NGSS state.** OLS-Science is an NGSS-influenced framework that retains Ohio-specific scope and a hybrid code system.

**Code format:** Standards are organized by individual grade with content statements and underlying syllabus elements. Topics include Earth and Space Science, Life Science, and Physical Science.

**Grade-level themes:**
- Grade 6 — Rocks, minerals, soil; cellular biology; matter and motion
- Grade 7 — **Cycles and patterns of Earth and the Moon; the hydrosphere and atmosphere — strong MHS overlap**
- Grade 8 — Forces and motion; species and reproduction; heredity

---

## OLS Standards Aligned to MHS Content

### Grade 7 — Cycles and Patterns of Earth and Sun, Hydrologic and Atmospheric Cycles *(STRONG match — Units 2–5)*

Grade 7 Earth and Space Science includes content statements on:
- The hydrologic cycle, including watersheds and human impact
- Composition and properties of the atmosphere
- Water as a renewable resource and its limited availability

These are direct MHS-content matches. Specific content statements address:
- The water cycle and its driving forces (sun energy, gravity)
- How water flows through watersheds
- Human impacts on water systems

**MHS objectives served:** `W2.1`, `W2.2`, `W3.1`, `W3.2`, `W4.1`–`W4.3`, `W5.1`, Unit 6 framing.

### Grade 6 — Rocks, Soils, and Earth's Surface

Grade 6 Earth and Space Science covers soil composition and how Earth's surface is shaped — adjacent to MHS Unit 4 (infiltration depends on soil) and Unit 2 (topography).

**MHS objectives served:** `W2.1`, `W4.1`–`W4.3` (adjacent).

### Scientific Inquiry Throughout

Each grade includes scientific-inquiry content statements covering data analysis, evidence-based reasoning, and argument construction. These are the MHS argumentation-arc touchpoints.

**MHS objectives served:** All argumentation objectives.

---

## Notable Differences from NGSS

1. **Strongly grade-7-anchored Earth science.** Most MHS-relevant content lives in grade 7, where NGSS distributes water-cycle content across MS and connects it to MS-PS1 (matter) and MS-ETS1 (engineering).
2. **No explicit three-dimensional structure** the way NGSS or STEELS use; SEPs and CCs are folded into content statements.
3. **No phase-change at molecular level** (NGSS MS-PS1-4) at depth — covered at macroscopic level only.

## Recommended Alignment Strategy

- **Grade 7 is the strongest fit.** Most of MHS's water-cycle content maps to grade 7 OLS Earth and Space Science.
- Use grade 6 for soil/topography framing.
- Use the scientific-inquiry strand across grades for argumentation alignment.

## Adoption & Status

- **Current standards adopted:** February 2018.
- **Implementation:** Phased through 2019; full implementation across all grades.
