# Wisconsin Standards for Science

## About

Wisconsin's current science standards are the **Wisconsin Standards for Science (WSS)**, adopted **November 2017** by the Wisconsin Department of Public Instruction (DPI). Standards are published by the [Wisconsin DPI](https://dpi.wi.gov/science/standards).

> Wisconsin DPI explicitly states that **WSS and NGSS are essentially equivalent** — districts may use either or both interchangeably and students will be well-prepared for the Wisconsin Forward Exam under either.

WSS uses three-dimensional learning (practices, core ideas, crosscutting concepts) in the K–2, 3–5, 6–8, 9–12 grade-band structure that NGSS uses.

## MHS Alignment

Because WSS is functionally equivalent to NGSS, the alignment in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** applies directly. Wisconsin teachers may cite either WSS or NGSS codes when claiming alignment.

## Adoption Details

- **Adopted:** November 2017 (Wisconsin DPI).
- **Equivalence to NGSS:** DPI explicitly affirms equivalence; districts may use NGSS in place of WSS.
- **Three-dimensional:** Yes.
- **Grade-band:** K–2, 3–5, 6–8, 9–12.
