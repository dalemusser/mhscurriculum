# Florida Next Generation Sunshine State Standards (NGSSS) — Science

## About

Florida's science standards are the **Next Generation Sunshine State Standards (NGSSS) for Science** — adopted February 2008 by the Florida State Board of Education, published by the [Florida Department of Education](https://www.fldoe.org/academics/standards/subject-areas/math-science/science/).

> ⚠️ **NGSSS is *not* NGSS.** The names are confusingly similar; Florida's "Next Generation Sunshine State Standards" predates and is independent of the national "Next Generation Science Standards." Florida has not adopted NGSS.

Florida is in the process of revising standards under the **Benchmarks for Excellent Student Thinking (B.E.S.T.)** initiative, but as of this writing science remains under NGSSS 2008.

**Code format:** `SC.<grade>.<body-of-knowledge>.<big-idea>.<benchmark>` — e.g. `SC.6.E.7.2`. Bodies of knowledge: N (Nature of Science), L (Life Science), P (Physical Science), E (Earth and Space Science).

**Big Ideas relevant to MHS:**
- E.6 — Earth in Space and Time
- E.7 — Earth Systems and Patterns (water cycle, atmosphere lives here)
- L.17 — Interdependence
- N.1 / N.2 / N.3 — Nature of Science

## NGSSS Standards Aligned to MHS Content

### SC.6.E.7 — The Water Cycle and Atmospheric Phenomena *(Unit 5 + general framing)*

Big Idea 7 standards under grade 6 cover the water cycle and atmospheric phenomena. Key benchmarks include water-cycle phase changes, the role of solar energy, and atmospheric layers. The most direct MHS-aligned benchmarks are within `SC.6.E.7.X` and address Unit 5 content (evaporation/condensation, sun-driven cycles).

**MHS objectives served:** `W5.1`, partial `W3.1`–`W4.3` (water-cycle framing).

### SC.7.E.6.6 — Human Impact on Earth's Surface

Earth and Space Science benchmarks at grade 7 include attention to how humans alter Earth's surface and resource use.

**MHS objectives served:** Unit 6 framing.

### SC.7.L.17 — Interdependence (Ecosystems)

Standards under Big Idea 17 address ecosystem interdependence and the effects of human activity on ecosystems — relevant to U3 pollution effects and U6 human impact.

### SC.6.N.1 / SC.7.N.1 / SC.8.N.1 — Practice of Science (Argumentation)

The Nature of Science standards under N.1 address scientific reasoning, evidence-based claims, and argumentation. These are the closest NGSSS equivalents to NGSS SEP 7.

**MHS objectives served:** All argumentation objectives (`A1.1`–`A5.1`).

## Notable Differences from NGSS

1. **Two-dimensional** rather than NGSS's three-dimensional structure — content + practices, with no explicit Crosscutting Concepts layer.
2. **No watershed-focused standard.** Florida's water-cycle coverage is at the atmospheric/global level; the watershed-scale flow that anchors MHS Units 2–3 is not directly addressed.
3. **No infiltration / groundwater-mechanics standard.** MHS Unit 4 content has no specific NGSSS analog.
4. **Argumentation is encoded in the Nature of Science strand**, not in content benchmarks the way NGSS distributes SEP 7.

## Recommended Alignment Strategy for Florida Deployment

- Lean on `SC.6.E.7.*` for Unit 5 alignment (water cycle).
- Lean on `SC.7.L.17.*` and `SC.7.E.6.6` for U3/U6 (human impact on ecosystems and surface).
- Use `SC.X.N.1.*` for the argumentation arc.
- Be transparent that MHS extends beyond NGSSS in groundwater modeling depth.

## Adoption & Status

- **Current standards:** NGSSS Science (adopted February 2008).
- **Revision:** B.E.S.T. for science is being developed; check FDOE for current status before making compliance claims.
