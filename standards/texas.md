# Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS) — Science

## About

The **Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS) for Science** are the science standards adopted by the Texas State Board of Education and published by the [Texas Education Agency (TEA)](https://tea.texas.gov/). The current K–8 science TEKS were given final approval **December 1, 2021** and replace the prior 2017 standards.

Texas is **not an NGSS state.** TEKS predates NGSS (originally adopted 1998) and follows a different structure. The 2021 revision incorporates some NGSS-style three-dimensional language ("Scientific and engineering practices") but the scope, sequence, and code system remain Texas-specific.

**Code format:** `SCIENCE.<grade>.<knowledge-and-skill-number>.<student-expectation-letter>` — e.g. `SCIENCE.7.11.A`. Each grade (6, 7, 8) is a separate set of ~10–13 numbered "Knowledge and Skills statements," each with lettered "Student Expectations." Standards are organized by **individual grade**, not by grade band.

**TEKS strands:**
- 6.1–6.5 / 7.1–7.5 / 8.1–8.5 — Scientific and engineering practices, data analysis, evidence-based explanations, science career connections, recurring themes
- 6.6+ — Matter and energy
- 7.x / 8.x — Force, motion, energy
- 8–11 — Earth and space
- 12+ — Organisms and environments

## TEKS Standards Aligned to MHS Content

Texas's coverage of MHS-relevant topics is **noticeably thinner than NGSS or MLS.** Several concepts central to MHS (the water cycle as a unifying model, watershed flow patterns, infiltration mechanics, phase change at the molecular level) are not explicitly addressed in 2021 TEKS at the depth NGSS requires. The standards below are the closest alignment opportunities.

### SCIENCE.6.10.A — Earth's Spheres *(Unit 2 framing)*

> **Differentiate between the biosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere, and geosphere and identify components of each system.**

**MHS objectives served:** Foundational framing for `W2.1` (topography), `W3.1`–`W4.3` (water systems), `W5.1` (atmosphere). MHS introduces the hydrosphere/atmosphere/geosphere conceptually through Toppo lessons.

---

### SCIENCE.6.11.A / 6.11.B — Resource Management *(broad U6 framing)*

> **6.11.A:** Research and describe why resource management is important in reducing global energy, poverty, malnutrition, and air and water pollution.
>
> **6.11.B:** Explain how conservation, increased efficiency, and technology can help manage air, water, soil, and energy resources.

**MHS objectives served:** Unit 6 (human impacts on water systems), the entire game framing (managing scarce water on a colonized planet).

**NGSS equivalents:** [MS-ESS3-1](ngss.md#ms-ess3-1--earths-resources), [MS-ESS3-3](ngss.md#ms-ess3-3--monitoring-and-minimizing-human-impact), [MS-ESS3-4](ngss.md#ms-ess3-4--population--per-capita-consumption)

---

### SCIENCE.7.11.A — Human Activity on Watersheds *(Unit 3 + Unit 6 — STRONG match)*

> **Analyze the beneficial and harmful influences of human activity on groundwater and surface water in a watershed.**

This is the single TEKS standard that most directly maps to MHS content. The grade 7 framing is "Earth and space. The student understands how human activity can impact the hydrosphere."

**MHS objectives served:** `W3.1` (predict spread of dissolved materials), `W3.2` (water flow direction), Unit 6 human impacts.
**MHS in-game activities:** U3 pollution sensor placement, U3 pollution argument, U3 seed planting, U6 final argumentation.

**NGSS equivalents:** [MS-ESS3-3](ngss.md#ms-ess3-3--monitoring-and-minimizing-human-impact), [MS-ESS3-4](ngss.md#ms-ess3-4--population--per-capita-consumption)

---

### SCIENCE.7.11.B — Human Activity on Ocean Systems *(adjacent — Unit 6)*

> **Describe human dependence and influence on ocean systems and explain how human activities impact these systems.**

**MHS adjacency:** Unit 6 (human impacts framing). MHS does not specifically address oceans, but the surface-water → groundwater → atmosphere cycle MHS teaches eventually connects to oceans.

---

### SCIENCE.8.10.A — Sun, Hydrosphere, Atmosphere Interaction *(Unit 5)*

> **Describe how energy from the Sun, hydrosphere, and atmosphere interact and influence weather and climate.**

**MHS objectives served:** `W5.1` (movement of water between surface and atmospheric systems). Unit 5's water cycle / evaporation / condensation lesson directly addresses sun-driven phase change driving the hydrologic cycle.

**NGSS equivalent:** [MS-ESS2-4](ngss.md#ms-ess2-4--cycling-of-water-through-earths-systems) (water cycle)

---

### SCIENCE.8.11.A / 8.11.B — Climate and Human Activity *(Unit 6)*

> **8.11.A:** Use scientific evidence to describe how natural events, including volcanic eruptions, meteor impacts, abrupt changes in ocean currents, and the release and absorption of greenhouse gases influence climate.
>
> **8.11.B:** Use scientific evidence to describe how human activities, including the release of greenhouse gases, deforestation, and urbanization, can influence climate.

**MHS objectives served:** Unit 6 (human impacts on atmospheric/water systems).

**NGSS equivalent:** [MS-ESS3-4](ngss.md#ms-ess3-4--population--per-capita-consumption) and [MS-ESS3-5](https://www.nextgenscience.org/pe/ms-ess3-5-earth-and-human-activity).

---

### SCIENCE.8.12.A — Disruptions to Ecosystems *(Unit 6)*

> **Explain how disruptions such as population changes, natural disasters, and human intervention impact the transfer of energy in food webs in ecosystems.**

**MHS adjacency:** Unit 6 ecosystem-disruption framing.

**NGSS equivalent:** [MS-LS2-4](ngss.md#ms-ls2-4--ecosystem-disruptions-affect-populations-strong-adjacency--unit-6)

### SCIENCE.6.1.A–H, 7.1.A–H, 8.1.A–H — Scientific and Engineering Practices

These are the **practice-level standards** that span all of MHS's argumentation arc. The relevant ones include:

- `SCIENCE.6.1.A` / `7.1.A` / `8.1.A`: ask questions and define problems
- `SCIENCE.6.1.B` / `7.1.B` / `8.1.B`: use scientific practices to plan and conduct investigations and use engineering practices to design solutions
- `SCIENCE.6.1.G` / `7.1.G` / `8.1.G`: develop and use models
- `SCIENCE.6.2.B` / `7.2.B` / `8.2.B`: analyze data
- `SCIENCE.6.3.A` / `7.3.A` / `8.3.A`: develop explanations and propose solutions supported by data and models
- `SCIENCE.6.3.C` / `7.3.C` / `8.3.C`: **engage respectfully in scientific argumentation using applied scientific explanations and empirical evidence**

The argumentation-engagement standard (`6.3.C` / `7.3.C` / `8.3.C`) is the **closest TEKS equivalent to NGSS SEP 7 (Engaging in Argument from Evidence)** and is the touchpoint for the entire MHS argumentation arc (`A1.1`–`A5.1`).

**MHS objectives served:** All argumentation objectives.

---

### SCIENCE.6.1.B / 7.1.B / 8.1.B — Engineering Design Practices

> Use scientific practices to plan and conduct descriptive, comparative, and experimental investigations and **use engineering practices to design solutions to problems.**

**MHS adjacency:** The Toppo Critique Side Quest (evaluating arguments as "design solutions" with criteria/constraints) lightly aligns here. MHS does not have a heavy engineering-design strand.

**NGSS equivalent:** [MS-ETS1-1](ngss.md#ms-ets1-1--defining-a-design-problem), [MS-ETS1-2](ngss.md#ms-ets1-2--evaluate-competing-design-solutions-strong-adjacency--argumentationcritique)

## Notable Differences from NGSS

1. **No explicit water cycle PE.** NGSS MS-ESS2-4 ("Develop a model to describe the cycling of water through Earth's systems...") has no direct TEKS equivalent. Texas covers parts of the water cycle in dispersed standards (`6.10.A` for spheres, `7.11.A` for human impact on watersheds, `8.10.A` for Sun-hydrosphere-atmosphere interaction) but does not require students to model the full hydrologic cycle as an integrated system.
2. **No phase-change / molecular-motion standard.** NGSS MS-PS1-4 (the perfect match for MHS Unit 5) has no TEKS equivalent at the middle-school level. Texas covers phase change at the macroscopic level only.
3. **No infiltration / groundwater-flow mechanics.** TEKS does not address infiltration rates or the saturated/unsaturated zone the way NGSS and MLS do (which is most of MHS Unit 4).
4. **Argumentation is a single SEP standard**, not a thread woven through content PEs. TEKS `X.3.C` is more general than NGSS SEP 7.
5. **Standards are by individual grade**, not by grade band. A teacher might select MHS for grade 7 (most water content lives there) or grade 8 (climate/human-impact content), but the alignment story differs depending on which grade they teach.
6. **Verb-driven SEP encoding.** Each TEKS standard's verb encodes the practice (model, analyze, describe, design); like MLS, dimensions are not separated.

## Recommended Alignment Strategy for Texas Deployment

If MHS is being adopted in Texas:

- **Grade 7 is the strongest fit** — `SCIENCE.7.11.A` (human activity on groundwater/surface water in a watershed) is the most direct claim. Pair with `SCIENCE.7.X.G` (modeling), `SCIENCE.7.3.C` (argumentation), and `SCIENCE.7.1.B` (engineering practices).
- **Grade 8** is a secondary fit for Unit 5 (water cycle / atmosphere) and Unit 6 (human impacts on climate) via `SCIENCE.8.10.A` and `SCIENCE.8.11.A/B`.
- **Grade 6** is mostly framing alignment (`6.10.A` spheres, `6.11.A/B` resource management).
- **Be explicit that MHS's water-cycle modeling (Unit 5) and infiltration mechanics (Unit 4) extend beyond TEKS requirements** — this is a strength, not a gap, but should be communicated to administrators reviewing for compliance.

## Adoption & Status

- **Current standards adopted:** December 1, 2021 (Texas State Board of Education).
- **Implementation:** First instructional use 2024–2025 school year for grades K–5; phased in for grades 6–12.
- **Administrative code reference:** [19 TAC Chapter 112](https://tea.texas.gov/about-tea/laws-and-rules/texas-administrative-code/19-tac-chapter-112).
- **Prior version:** 2017 TEKS (still in use during 2021 transition).
