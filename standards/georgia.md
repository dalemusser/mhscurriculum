# Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) — Science

## About

Georgia's current science standards are the **Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) for Science**, adopted in 2016 (replacing Georgia Performance Standards). Standards are published by the [Georgia Department of Education](https://www.georgiastandards.org/Georgia-Standards/Pages/Science.aspx).

Georgia is **not an NGSS state.** GSE is NGSS-influenced but retains Georgia-specific scope and code system.

**Code format:** `S<grade><strand><number>` — e.g. `S6E1` reads "Science, grade 6, Earth Science, standard 1." Sub-elaborations follow as `.a`, `.b`, etc.

**Strands:** P (Physical Science), L (Life Science), E (Earth Science).

**Grade-level themes (relevant ones):**
- Grade 6 — Earth Science (`S6E1`–`S6E6`); covers solar system, Earth's structure, weather, water cycle
- Grade 7 — Life Science (less MHS overlap)
- Grade 8 — Physical Science (less MHS overlap)

---

## GSE Standards Aligned to MHS Content

### S6E3 — Water Distribution and Movement *(STRONG match — Units 2, 3, 5)*

Grade 6 Earth Science standard `S6E3` addresses water distribution on Earth, the water cycle, and watershed flow. Sub-elaborations cover:
- Water cycle including evaporation, condensation, precipitation
- Watersheds and water flow
- Human use and impacts on water systems

**MHS objectives served:** `W2.1`, `W2.2`, `W3.1`, `W3.2`, `W5.1`, partial Unit 6.

### S6E5 — Earth's Surface, Soil, and Atmosphere *(Units 2, 4, 5)*

Earth's surface processes and atmospheric phenomena. Includes soil composition (relevant to U4 infiltration) and atmospheric layers (U5).

**MHS objectives served:** `W4.1`–`W4.3`, `W5.1` (adjacent).

### S6E6 — Renewable and Nonrenewable Resources *(Unit 6)*

Resource use and conservation, with focus on water as a critical resource.

**MHS objectives served:** Unit 6 framing.

### Scientific Practices Across Grades

Georgia GSE includes "Characteristics of Science" / "Science and Engineering Practices" sections that map to argumentation. Specific to argumentation:
- `S6/7/8.CS5` (or equivalent) — characteristics of scientific knowledge include argumentation supported by evidence

**MHS objectives served:** All argumentation objectives.

---

## Notable Differences from NGSS

1. **Heavily grade-6-anchored** for Earth science. Most MHS-relevant content lives in grade 6.
2. **No groundwater-mechanics standard** at NGSS-level depth.
3. **Phase change covered at macroscopic level**, not molecular.

## Recommended Alignment Strategy

- **Grade 6 is the strongest fit** — `S6E3` (water cycle/watersheds) is the central anchor.
- Use `S6E5` and `S6E6` for U4/U6.
- Use the SEP-equivalent characteristics standards for argumentation.

## Adoption & Status

- **Current standards:** GSE adopted 2016, replacing Georgia Performance Standards.
- **Note:** GeorgiaStandards.org is being sunset; standards content is migrating to GADOE platforms — verify URLs.
