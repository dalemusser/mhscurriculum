# Tennessee Academic Standards for Science

## About

Tennessee's current science standards are the **Tennessee Academic Standards for Science**, adopted **October 2022** by the Tennessee State Board of Education and **implemented in classrooms beginning the 2025–2026 school year**. Standards are published by the [Tennessee Department of Education](https://www.tn.gov/education/districts/academic-standards/science-standards.html) (with documentation hosted at [Best for All Central](https://bestforall.tnedu.gov/academic-standards/science)).

Tennessee was a **lead state in NGSS development** and the 2022 standards are heavily NGSS-influenced — they are explicitly built on the *Framework for K-12 Science Education* (NRC, 2012) and incorporate three-dimensional learning. Tennessee did not formally adopt NGSS but the alignment is close.

**Code format:** Tennessee uses a `<grade>.<DCI-domain>.<number>` format with disciplinary-core-idea organization — e.g. `6.ESS2.4` (similar enough to MLS that the mapping is straightforward).

**Grade 6 focus (per Tennessee documentation):** "How energy, found in multiple systems and scales, drives ecosystems (populations, food chains/webs), Earth's natural resources, and Earth processes (oceans, weather, and climate)."

---

## TAS-Science Standards Aligned to MHS Content

Because Tennessee's 2022 standards mirror the NGSS three-dimensional structure and disciplinary core ideas, the MHS alignment essentially follows the NGSS mapping in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** with Tennessee-specific code numbers.

The 2022 standards address:
- Earth's water systems (water cycle, watersheds, atmosphere) — middle school
- Human impacts on Earth systems
- Engineering design and scientific argumentation

Tennessee codes in the 6.ESS / 6.PS / 6.LS / 6.ETS pattern map closely to NGSS PEs and Missouri MLS codes.

**MHS objectives served:** All MHS objectives covered by NGSS apply to Tennessee analogs.

---

## Notable Differences from NGSS

Tennessee's standards retain NGSS's three-dimensional structure but are **organized by individual grade** (not by grade band), reflecting Tennessee's preference for tighter per-grade scope. The disciplinary-core-idea organization (DCI-leading codes) mirrors the structure used by Missouri MLS.

## Recommended Alignment Strategy

- The NGSS PE-by-PE mapping in `ngss.md` applies directly; substitute Tennessee codes for the corresponding NGSS PE codes when claiming alignment for Tennessee compliance.
- Verify exact Tennessee code-to-PE mappings against the [official 2022 standards document](https://www.tn.gov/content/dam/tn/stateboardofeducation/documents/standards/science/New%2010-28-22%20Science%20Standards.pdf) before publishing alignment claims.

## Adoption & Status

- **Current standards adopted:** October 2022 (Tennessee State Board of Education).
- **Implementation:** Classroom use beginning 2025–2026.
- **Prior version:** Tennessee Academic Standards for Science (2016).
