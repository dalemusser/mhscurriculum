# California Next Generation Science Standards (CA NGSS)

## About

California adopted the **California Next Generation Science Standards (CA NGSS)** in **2013**. CA NGSS is the unmodified NGSS three-dimensional standards adopted by the California State Board of Education, published by the [California Department of Education](https://www.cde.ca.gov/pd/ca/sc/ngssstandards.asp).

**Code format:** Standard NGSS — `MS-ESS2-4`, `MS-ESS3-1`, etc. California uses the NGSS PE codes verbatim.

## MHS Alignment

CA NGSS is NGSS. The MHS alignment is fully documented in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** — the 5 directly cited PEs, 5 adjacent PEs, and the argumentation/SEP-7 mapping all apply to California identically.

## Adoption Details

- **Adopted:** September 4, 2013 (State Board of Education)
- **Implementation:** Phased; full statewide implementation by 2018–2019
- **Local name:** California Next Generation Science Standards (CA NGSS)
- **Modifications from NGSS:** None at the PE level. California has published its own *Science Framework for California Public Schools* that organizes NGSS PEs into recommended grade-by-grade sequences (the "Preferred Integrated Course Model" for middle school groups PEs by grade 6, 7, and 8 rather than treating 6–8 as a single band).
- **Implication for MHS:** A California middle-school teacher will see PEs assigned to specific grades. MHS aligns to grade-band 6–8 PEs that California has distributed across grades 6, 7, and 8 — alignment claims should reference the PE itself, not the grade.
