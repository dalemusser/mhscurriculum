# North Carolina Standard Course of Study (NCSCOS) — Science

## About

North Carolina's science standards are the **NC Standard Course of Study for Science** ("Essential Standards" for science). The current standards were **approved by the North Carolina State Board of Education in 2023**, with required implementation in 2024–2025. Standards are published by the [NC Department of Public Instruction (DPI)](https://www.dpi.nc.gov/teach-nc/curriculum-instruction/standard-course-study/science).

North Carolina is **not an NGSS state.** The 2023 NCSCOS revision is NGSS-influenced and adopts three-dimensional approaches but retains NC-specific code structure and scope.

**Code format:** `<grade>.<strand>.<number>.<sub-number>` — e.g. `8.E.1.1`. Strands: P (Physical Science), L (Life Science), E (Earth/Environmental Science).

**Grade-level themes:**
- Grade 6 — Mixed (Earth and Space, Physical Science)
- Grade 7 — Life Science
- Grade 8 — Physical Science with **water quality, water treatment, and the hydrosphere** as Earth Science focal points

---

## NCSCOS Standards Aligned to MHS Content

### Grade 8 — Hydrosphere, Water Quality, Water Treatment *(STRONG match)*

The grade 8 Earth Science strand at NC includes standards on:
- The composition and properties of water in the hydrosphere
- Water quality and how human activity affects it
- Water treatment processes

**MHS objectives served:** `W3.1` (pollution spread), `W3.2` (water flow), Unit 6 (human impacts).

### Grade 6 — Earth Systems and Resources

Grade 6 Earth Science covers Earth's spheres, weather, and resource use — adjacent to MHS Units 2, 5, 6.

**MHS objectives served:** Foundational framing for `W2.1`, `W5.1`, Unit 6.

### Science Practices

NCSCOS includes science-practice expectations across grades that cover argumentation, modeling, and evidence-based reasoning.

**MHS objectives served:** All argumentation objectives.

---

## Notable Differences from NGSS

1. **Hydrosphere content lives at grade 8** in NC; NGSS distributes water content across MS as a band.
2. **Engineering not as integrated** — NCSCOS treats engineering as adjacent rather than woven into science standards the way NGSS does.

## Recommended Alignment Strategy

- **Grade 8 Earth Science is the strongest fit** for U3 and Unit 6.
- Earlier grades work for foundational framing.
- Use practice-oriented standards for the argumentation arc.

## Adoption & Status

- **Current standards:** Approved 2023 (NC State Board of Education).
- **Implementation:** Required 2024–2025 school year.
- **Prior version:** NC Essential Standards for Science (2010).
