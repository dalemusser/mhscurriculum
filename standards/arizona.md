# Arizona Science Standards (AzSS)

## About

Arizona's current science standards are the **Arizona Science Standards (AzSS)**, adopted **2018** by the Arizona State Board of Education. Standards are published by the [Arizona Department of Education](https://www.azed.gov/standards-practices/k-12standards/standards-science).

Arizona is **not formally an NGSS state**, but AzSS and NGSS were both designed using the *Framework for K-12 Science Education* (NRC, 2012), and AzSS uses the same **three-dimensional structure** (Science and Engineering Practices, Crosscutting Concepts, Core Ideas).

Arizona was a [lead state in NGSS development](https://www.nextgenscience.org/lead-state-arizona) but ultimately published its own state-branded standards rather than adopting NGSS verbatim.

## MHS Alignment

AzSS is structurally and substantively close to NGSS. Arizona publishes its own AzSS-NGSS correlation summaries; the MHS alignment in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** applies via that crosswalk.

## Adoption Details

- **Adopted:** 2018 (Arizona State Board of Education).
- **Three-dimensional:** Yes.
- **AzSS vs NGSS:** Arizona explicitly maintains its own standards branding, but provides correlation tables.
