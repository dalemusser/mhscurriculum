# Massachusetts Science and Technology/Engineering (STE) Curriculum Framework

## About

Massachusetts's current science standards are the **2016 Massachusetts Science and Technology/Engineering (STE) Curriculum Framework**, adopted **April 2016** by the Massachusetts Board of Elementary and Secondary Education. Standards are published by the [Massachusetts Department of Elementary and Secondary Education (DESE)](https://www.doe.mass.edu/frameworks/scitech/).

Massachusetts was a [lead state in NGSS development](https://www.nextgenscience.org/lead-state-massachusetts), and the 2016 STE Framework is **NGSS-derived but separately developed**, not a direct NGSS adoption. The MA framework integrates engineering more heavily than NGSS does (it's "Science and Technology/Engineering," not "Science").

**Code format:** `<grade>.<topic>.<DCI>-<number>` for content; eight Science and Engineering Practices integrated across grades. Code format differs from NGSS PE codes — MA uses its own.

**Grade-band structure:** PreK–2, 3–5, 6–8 (matches NGSS grade-band approach for middle school).

## STE Framework Content Aligned to MHS

Massachusetts middle school (6–8) covers:
- Earth's water cycle, watersheds, and human impacts (similar coverage to NGSS MS-ESS2-4, MS-ESS3-1, MS-ESS3-3, MS-ESS3-4)
- Phase change at the molecular level (similar to NGSS MS-PS1-4)
- Engineering design and argumentation (similar to NGSS MS-ETS1 and SEP 7)

The MA standards are **substantively very close to NGSS** for MHS-relevant topics; the divergence is primarily in code numbering and Massachusetts-specific framing of engineering integration.

## MHS Alignment

The five NGSS PEs cited by MHS, the five adjacent PEs, and the argumentation/SEP-7 thread documented in **[ngss.md](ngss.md)** all have direct MA STE equivalents. For MA-specific code references, consult the [2016 STE Standards document](https://www.doe.mass.edu/frameworks/scitech/2016-04/STE-Standards.pdf).

## Adoption Details

- **Adopted:** April 2016 (MA Board of Elementary and Secondary Education).
- **Local name:** Massachusetts Science and Technology/Engineering Curriculum Framework.
- **Lead state in NGSS:** Yes — Massachusetts participated in NGSS drafting.
- **Modifications from NGSS:** Code structure differs; engineering integration is more pronounced; some MA-specific content emphasis.
- **Three-dimensional:** Yes — explicitly integrates the eight NGSS Science and Engineering Practices.
