# Mismatches: MHS Implementation vs. States with Non-NGSS Standards

This document calls out the places where Mission HydroSci's curriculum either **exceeds what a state's standards require** or **fails to address something a state requires**, focused on states whose science standards differ substantively from NGSS.

For NGSS or NGSS-equivalent states (~22 states + DC, plus most of the NGSS-influenced states), MHS aligns in substance even when the local code numbers differ — see [`alignment-matrix.md`](alignment-matrix.md) and [`standards/ngss.md`](standards/ngss.md). Those alignments are not repeated here.

The states covered below are the ones whose science frameworks were not built on NGSS or were built before NGSS:

| State | Framework | Relationship to NGSS |
|---|---|---|
| **Texas** | TEKS 2021 | Predates NGSS; structurally different; comparatively thin coverage of MHS topics |
| **Florida** | NGSSS 2008 | Predates NGSS (despite the confusingly similar name) |
| **Virginia** | SOL 2018 | Independent framework; strong watershed match but other gaps |
| **Georgia** | GSE 2016 | NGSS-influenced but distinct; less depth |
| **Ohio** | OLS 2018 | NGSS-influenced but distinct; concentrates Earth content in grade 7 |
| **Mississippi** | MS CCRS 2018 | Distinct; less detail in Earth/water content |
| **Missouri** | MLS 2016 | Heavily NGSS-derived; only minor mismatches (relevant because MHS docs cite MLS codes natively) |

For each state below: **MHS Exceeds** = MHS content the state's standards do not require; **State Gaps** = state requirements MHS does not address; **Deployment notes** = practical guidance.

---

## Texas (TEKS 2021) — Largest Mismatch

Texas standards are organized by **individual grade**, are not three-dimensional, and have measurably less coverage of MHS-relevant topics than NGSS or NGSS-derived state frameworks.

### MHS Exceeds Texas

| MHS content | Why TEKS doesn't require it |
|---|---|
| **Full water cycle as an integrated model** (Units 2–5; NGSS MS-ESS2-4) | TEKS has no PE asking students to model the hydrologic cycle as a whole. Coverage is split across `6.10.A` (Earth's spheres), `7.11.A` (human impact on a watershed), and `8.10.A` (sun-hydrosphere-atmosphere) without integration. |
| **Particle-level model of phase change** (Unit 5; NGSS MS-PS1-4) | TEKS covers phase change at the macroscopic level only. No equivalent of MS-PS1-4. |
| **Infiltration mechanics — soil pore size, saturated/unsaturated zones, water table** (Unit 4) | TEKS does not address infiltration rates or the mechanics of water moving through soil layers. |
| **Argumentation as a unit-end synthesis activity** (every unit) | TEKS argumentation is one general SEP standard (`X.3.C`) — not woven through content the way SEP 7 does in NGSS. |
| **Watershed-scale flow tracing** (Units 2–3) | TEKS only addresses watershed in `7.11.A` (human-impact framing). |

### Texas Gaps in MHS

| Texas requirement | MHS coverage |
|---|---|
| `6.10.B` (model layers of Earth: inner core, outer core, mantle, crust) | Not addressed. |
| `6.10.C` (rock cycle and rock formation) | Not addressed. |
| `7.10` (plate tectonics, including supervolcanoes/hot spots) | Not addressed. |
| `7.12` (energy flow through trophic levels, food webs) | Not addressed. |
| `8.10.C` (ocean currents and tropical cyclones) | Not addressed. |
| `8.11.A/B` (climate, greenhouse gases, deforestation) | Touched lightly in Unit 6; less depth than required. |

### Deployment Notes — Texas

- **Best fit is grade 7.** `SCIENCE.7.11.A` ("analyze the beneficial and harmful influences of human activity on groundwater and surface water in a watershed") is the most direct standard MHS aligns to.
- **Be transparent about the depth gap.** When pitching MHS to a Texas district, frame the water-cycle modeling and infiltration mechanics as enrichment beyond TEKS, not as TEKS coverage.
- **MHS does not replace a Texas Earth-science unit.** Pair MHS with grade-7 supplements addressing plate tectonics, food webs, and ocean systems.

## Florida (NGSSS 2008) — Pre-NGSS Framework

Florida's NGSSS predates NGSS by five years and is two-dimensional (content + practices, no Crosscutting Concepts layer).

### MHS Exceeds Florida

| MHS content | Why NGSSS doesn't require it |
|---|---|
| **Watershed-scale water flow** (Units 2–3) | NGSSS water-cycle coverage is at the atmospheric/global level. No watershed-flow standard. |
| **Infiltration mechanics, water table, soil pore size** (Unit 4) | No NGSSS standard at this depth. |
| **Modeling phase change at the molecular/particle level** (Unit 5) | NGSSS covers phase change macroscopically only. |
| **Argumentation woven through content** (every unit) | NGSSS argumentation is in the Nature of Science strand, not distributed through content benchmarks. |

### Florida Gaps in MHS

| Florida requirement | MHS coverage |
|---|---|
| `SC.6.E.6` Earth in Space and Time (solar system, planets) | Not addressed. |
| `SC.7.E.6` Earth's surface processes including weathering, erosion | Not directly addressed; MHS uses topography but not the weathering/erosion mechanisms. |
| `SC.6.L.14` / `SC.7.L.16` Heredity and reproduction | Not addressed (life-science strands outside MHS scope). |

### Deployment Notes — Florida

- **Strongest hooks are `SC.6.E.7.*` (water cycle / atmosphere) and `SC.7.L.17` (interdependence/human impact on ecosystems).**
- **Use `SC.X.N.1` (Nature of Science / argumentation) for the argumentation arc.**
- **Florida is moving to B.E.S.T. standards.** Verify current status with FDOE before publishing alignment claims.

## Virginia (SOL 2018) — Strong on Watersheds, Gaps Elsewhere

Virginia's `6.8` standard ("land and water have roles in watershed systems") is one of the strongest single-standard fits for MHS in any U.S. framework — unusually well-aligned to MHS Units 2–3. The mismatches are in other areas.

### MHS Exceeds Virginia

| MHS content | Why SOL doesn't require it |
|---|---|
| **Infiltration mechanics, soil pore size, water table** (Unit 4) | SOL does not require this depth on groundwater mechanics. |
| **Particle-level model of phase change** (Unit 5) | SOL atmospheric content (`6.7`) is at conventional depth, not particle-level. |
| **Argumentation as a unit-end synthesis activity** (every unit) | SOL argumentation lives in the Scientific Investigation strand (`X.1`), not woven through content. |

### Virginia Gaps in MHS

| Virginia requirement | MHS coverage |
|---|---|
| `6.4` Matter and energy / atomic structure | Not addressed. |
| `6.6` Solar system | Not addressed. |
| `7.X` Life science (cells, organisms) | Not addressed. |
| `8.7` / `8.8` Earth and space (rocks, plate tectonics, oceans) | Partially touched (topography), not at SOL depth. |

### Deployment Notes — Virginia

- **Grade 6 is the strongest fit, anchored on `6.8` (watersheds) plus `6.7` (atmosphere) and `6.9` (resource conservation).**
- The argumentation arc maps cleanly to `6.1` / `7.1` / `8.1` (Scientific Investigation, Reasoning, and Logic).

## Georgia (GSE 2016) — Heavily Grade-6 Anchored

Georgia GSE concentrates Earth-science content in grade 6 (`S6E1`–`S6E6`). Grade 7 is life science and grade 8 is physical science.

### MHS Exceeds Georgia

| MHS content | Why GSE doesn't require it |
|---|---|
| **Particle-level phase change** (Unit 5) | GSE phase-change coverage is macroscopic. |
| **Infiltration / groundwater mechanics** (Unit 4) | No GSE standard at NGSS depth. |
| **Cross-unit argumentation thread** (`A1.1`–`A5.1`) | GSE argumentation lives in characteristics-of-science framing, not distributed through content. |

### Georgia Gaps in MHS

| Georgia requirement | MHS coverage |
|---|---|
| `S6E1`–`S6E2` Solar system, Earth-Moon-Sun | Not addressed. |
| `S6E5` (parts beyond water/soil) — minerals, rocks, layers of Earth | Not addressed. |
| `S7L*` (full life science strand) | Not addressed. |
| `S8P*` (physical science: matter, motion, forces) | Not addressed. |

### Deployment Notes — Georgia

- **MHS aligns best with grade 6 (`S6E3` water distribution and movement is the central anchor).**
- Pair with grade 7 life-science and grade 8 physical-science instruction; MHS is not a full middle-school science replacement in Georgia.

## Ohio (OLS 2018) — Earth Content Concentrated in Grade 7

Ohio places most of the MHS-relevant Earth-science content in grade 7. Grade 6 has soils/rocks; grade 8 covers physical/biological topics.

### MHS Exceeds Ohio

| MHS content | Why OLS doesn't require it |
|---|---|
| **Particle-level phase change** (Unit 5) | OLS phase-change coverage is macroscopic. |
| **Infiltration mechanics and soil pore size as a model** (Unit 4) | OLS grade 6 covers soils descriptively; not at NGSS-style modeling depth. |
| **Cross-unit argumentation thread** | OLS argumentation lives in scientific-inquiry content statements within each grade. |

### Ohio Gaps in MHS

| Ohio requirement | MHS coverage |
|---|---|
| Grade 6 — rocks, minerals, geologic processes | Not addressed. |
| Grade 6 — cellular biology | Not addressed. |
| Grade 8 — forces and motion, heredity | Not addressed. |

### Deployment Notes — Ohio

- **Grade 7 is by far the strongest fit.** Most of MHS's water-cycle content maps to OLS grade 7 Earth and Space Science.
- **Grade 6 is a partial fit** for soil/topography framing but does not satisfy the rest of the OLS grade-6 expectations.

## Mississippi (MS CCRS 2018) — Less Detailed Than NGSS

Mississippi's standards are organized into three content strands (Life, Physical, Earth/Space) with comparatively less granular detail on water-system content than NGSS or NGSS-derived states.

### MHS Exceeds Mississippi

| MHS content | Why MS CCRS doesn't require it |
|---|---|
| **Infiltration mechanics, water table** (Unit 4) | Not addressed at MHS depth. |
| **Particle-level phase change** (Unit 5) | Not addressed at MHS depth. |
| **Watershed-flow tracing as an integrated model** | Touched conceptually, not at MHS depth. |

### Mississippi Gaps in MHS

The MS CCRS Life Science and Physical Science strands cover content (cells, heredity, matter/motion) that MHS does not address.

### Deployment Notes — Mississippi

- **Use the Earth and Space Science strand at grade 6–8 for alignment claims.** Verify exact code references against the [2018 MS CCRS document](https://www.pearson.com/content/dam/global-store/en-us/schools/states/Mississippi/2018MississippiScienceStandardsConceptualIntegratedScience.pdf).

## Missouri (MLS 2016) — Minor Mismatches

Missouri is the case where MHS curriculum docs cite the codes natively (MHS team is at the University of Missouri). MLS is heavily NGSS-derived; mismatches are minor and structural rather than substantive.

### Mismatches between MLS and the NGSS framing MHS uses

| MLS code | MLS wording | NGSS equivalent | Mismatch |
|---|---|---|---|
| `6-8.ESS3.A.1` | Adds **"and human activity"** as a cause of uneven distribution | MS-ESS3-1 attributes only to geoscience processes | Substantive: MLS includes a human-cause framing NGSS doesn't. |
| `6-8.ESS3.C.1` | Asks students to **"Analyze data"** | MS-ESS3-4 asks them to **"Construct an argument"** | Substantive: NGSS argumentation framing fits MHS better; MLS's data-analysis framing is a different SEP. |
| MLS standards in general | Three dimensions (SEP, DCI, CC) **not listed explicitly** on each standard; SEP encoded in the verb | NGSS lists all three dimensions per PE | Cosmetic: matters for LLM prompting and three-dimensional teaching, not for content alignment. |

### Missouri Gaps in MHS

None substantive — every MLS standard cited by MHS docs is either fully implemented or partially implemented in the game.

### Deployment Notes — Missouri

- **MLS is the native code language for MHS.** If a Missouri teacher cites MLS codes, the MHS docs use those exact codes.
- **For LLM evaluation prompts**, prefer the NGSS three-dimensional structure (in [`standards/ngss.md`](standards/ngss.md)) over the MLS structure since NGSS makes the SEP/DCI/CC roles explicit.

## Cross-Cutting Patterns

Looking across the seven states above, the same MHS strengths and gaps recur:

### MHS Consistently Exceeds Non-NGSS States In:

1. **Particle-level modeling of phase change** (Unit 5). Only NGSS-aligned states require this; FL, GA, MS, OH, TX, VA all cover phase change macroscopically.
2. **Infiltration mechanics and water-table depth content** (Unit 4). FL, GA, MS, OH, TX, VA do not address this at MHS depth.
3. **Argumentation as a content-integrated thread** (every unit). Most non-NGSS states keep argumentation in a separate "Nature of Science" or "Scientific Investigation" strand rather than weaving it through content.
4. **Modeling the water cycle as an integrated system** (Units 2–5). Only NGSS-aligned states require students to integrate the cycle as a whole; non-NGSS states tend to fragment it.

### MHS Consistently Does Not Address:

1. **Solar system, Earth–Moon–Sun, plate tectonics, rocks/minerals.** Common in non-NGSS state grade 6 standards.
2. **Cellular biology, genetics, heredity.** Common in grade 7 of non-NGSS states.
3. **Forces, motion, atomic structure.** Common in grade 8 of non-NGSS states.

This means **MHS is best positioned as a water-and-argumentation supplement** in non-NGSS states, not as a standalone middle-school science curriculum. The deeper, more integrated NGSS framing in MHS is a strength when the state allows enrichment beyond required standards, and a positioning challenge when administrators need a one-to-one match against state-required content.

## How to Use This Document

- **For state-deployment decisions:** if MHS is being considered in any of the seven states above, this document tells the curriculum coordinator what they will need to supplement and what MHS adds beyond their state's requirements.
- **For dashboard / instructor messaging:** when surfacing MHS content to a teacher in one of these states, prefer phrasings tied to the state's standards (e.g., reference Texas `7.11.A` rather than NGSS `MS-ESS3-3` for a Texas teacher). The per-state files in [`standards/`](standards/) have the local code reference.
- **For LLM evaluation:** the LLM prompts should ground evaluation in NGSS dimensions (clearer structure) but **the alignment claim presented to a teacher** should use the state-specific code from this document.
