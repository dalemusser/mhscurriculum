### What's driving the disagreement

The team's `Identification` vs. `Arg Alignment` line appears to be:

- **Identification** = the source argument is **already articulated in writing** (Matt, Cathy, David, Sarah, Kelly say their claim/evidence/reasoning out loud); the student picks the right sentence out of the written argument.
- **Arg Alignment** = the source is a **representation/picture**, and the student has to **infer** a claim or evidence that aligns with the picture.

That distinction is not present in the codebook (the codebook tags every item in the disagreement zone with the single label `Identification`); the team appears to be applying a finer-grained categorization than the codebook itself records.

My split was different: I lumped all "what is the claim/evidence/reasoning?" stems together as **Structure** (regardless of whether the source was a written argument or a picture) and reserved **Alignment** for the explicit evidence-↔-claim matching items (item 25's matrix and the "which contradicts the representation?" items 39 and 49).

Both splits are internally consistent under their respective definitions. The team's split tracks the **input format** (written text vs. representation) of the source argument; my split tracks the **task type** (identify a part vs. match evidence to a claim).

### The one apparent inconsistency in the team's categorization

Items **39** and **49** are structurally near-identical: both ask "which [statement/evidence] contradicts [a character]'s representation?"

- Item 39 (S3Q15): "Which **statement** contradicts Laura's representation?"
- Item 49 (S3Q25): "Which **evidence** contradicts Amanda's representation?"

The team places item 49 in **Arg Alignment** but item 39 in **Identification**. The only surface difference is that item 39 says "statement" while item 49 says "evidence" — a thin distinction for a categorization line. This may be deliberate (the team may treat item 39 as testing whether the student can *recognize* a contradicting statement, while item 49 explicitly frames the contradicting item as *evidence*), or it may be a categorization slip worth confirming with the team. In my own answer I placed both 39 and 49 in **Alignment** to reflect their parallel structure.

## Recommendation

For downstream use (dashboard messaging, LLM evaluation prompts, supplemental-activity targeting), the **curriculum team's categorization should be the authoritative one**, since the team owns the construct definitions. The points to confirm with the team are:

1. **Item 39 vs. item 49** — confirm whether the difference in placement is intentional (treating "statement" and "evidence" as different task types) or whether item 39 should also live in Arg Alignment.
2. **The Identification / Arg Alignment line** — confirm the working definition (suggested above: written-argument source = Identification; picture/representation source = Arg Alignment), since this distinction is not encoded in the codebook tags themselves and would otherwise need to be re-derived per item by reading the stem.
