**This document responds to the following question:**

> The water systems assessment instrument has items for groundwater, water cycle, surface water and watersheds including topography.
>
> The argumentation assessment instrument has items for alignment, structure and critiques.
>
> Can you give me a list of the question numbers that fit each of the item types on the 2 assessments?

---

> **Notes on the categorization** — two things to flag in case you'd categorize them differently:
>
> 1. **Atmospheric water** is a separate codebook tag — I rolled it into water cycle to match the four-category framing, but it could stand alone.
> 2. **Alignment vs. structure** isn't in the codebook (both are tagged `Identification`); I split them by reading each item stem. Items 25, 39, 49 are the ones that ask the student to *match* evidence to a claim or representation rather than identify which part of the argument is which.
>
> Pre-test and post-test are identical in items, numbering, and tags, so this applies to both.

## Argumentation Assessment (Section 3, items 25–51)

The codebook tags Section 3 items with two sub-labels: `Identification` and `Critique/Evaluation`. The `Identification` tag covers two distinct activities, which the rubric below splits per the user's three-way framing:

- **Alignment** — match evidence to the claim it supports (or identify which evidence contradicts a representation). Codebook-literal `Identification`.
- **Structure** — identify a specific part of an argument (claim / evidence / reasoning). Codebook-literal `Identification`.
- **Critique** — evaluate which of two arguments is stronger and articulate why. Codebook-literal `Critique/Evaluation`.

## Notes on the Categorization

1. **Atmospheric water → water cycle.** The codebook keeps `atmospheric water` (evaporation, condensation, particle-level phase change) as a distinct sub-tag from `water cycle`. Because evaporation and condensation are the surface↔atmosphere legs of the water cycle (and are also the substance of MHS objective W5.1, "explain movement of water between surface and atmospheric systems"), they are folded into the water-cycle category in this document. The literal codebook tag is preserved in the per-item table for traceability.

2. **Identification → alignment vs. structure.** The codebook collapses two argumentation sub-skills under the single tag `Identification`: (a) aligning evidence with the claim it supports — including identifying evidence that *contradicts* a representation — and (b) identifying which sentence in a written argument is the claim, the evidence, or the reasoning. The split applied here was made by reading the item stem, not by inspecting any codebook field.

3. **Pre/post symmetry.** The pre-test and post-test use the same items in the same order with the same codebook tags, so this index applies identically to both.

4. **Item 25 (S3Q1)** is a four-row matrix item; the codebook decomposes it into S3Q1_1 through S3Q1_4. Counted as one survey question above.
